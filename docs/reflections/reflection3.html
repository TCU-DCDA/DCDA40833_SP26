Reflection 3

Final Reflection

Semester synthesis and grade argument (Weeks 9-16)

Overview

This reflection asks you to assess your development over the entire semester, with particular emphasis on Weeks 9-16, and argue for a grade for the second half of the course.

In keeping with this course's ungrading philosophy, your final course grade is the average of two self-assigned grades:

  • Midsemester reflection (covered Weeks 1-8)
  • This final reflection (covers Weeks 9-16)

Your Skills Portfolio and Semester Project serve as the primary evidence for this reflection. Use these completed works to demonstrate your growth and mastery of DCDA proficiencies.

Important: Late Final Reflections are NOT accepted (graduating seniors). This is a firm deadline.

Overview

Instructions: Write an overview (2-3 paragraphs) of your development over the entire semester. How did you grow from Week 1 to Week 16? What were your most significant accomplishments? What challenges did you overcome?

Proficiency Strands

Address each of the following strands with specific examples from your work throughout the semester:

Digital Culture

Instructions: Describe how you've developed digital culture skills throughout the entire semester. Reference specific examples from both your portfolio and semester project (web development, digital design, multimodal authoring, digital communication, etc.).

Example evidence: Skills Portfolio (Labs 1-6), Semester Project (Option 2 focus), AI tool integration

Data Analytics

Instructions: Describe how you've developed data analytics skills throughout the semester. Reference specific examples from your portfolio and project work (data collection, analysis, visualization, interpretation, storytelling).

Example evidence: Labs 3-4-6 (Critique, Tableau, Mapping), Semester Project (Option 1 focus)

Project Design and Planning

Instructions: Describe how you approached planning and executing a long-term project. What was your methodology? How did you manage scope, time, and deliverables? What would you do differently?

Example evidence: Semester Project planning, execution, and iteration

Collaboration

Instructions: Describe how you engaged with instructors, peers, and feedback throughout the semester. How did you incorporate feedback? How did you contribute to class discussions or help others?

Example evidence: Response to feedback, class participation, peer collaboration (if applicable)

Self-Evaluation

Instructions: Provide an honest evaluation of your work during Weeks 9-16 and the semester as a whole. Consider:

  • Quality and completeness of your Semester Project
  • Final state of your Skills Portfolio
  • Timeliness of submissions
  • Engagement with course material and feedback
  • Growth from your Baseline Reflection to now
  • Areas where you excelled and areas for continued growth
  • Any missing or incomplete assignments

Grade Argument

Instructions: Based on the evidence above, argue for the grade you believe you've earned for Weeks 9-16 (the second half of the semester). Be specific and reference your work.

Format: "Based on the above information, I argue that I have earned a(n) [A/B/C/D/F] for the second half of the semester because..."

Final Grade Calculation: Your final course grade will be the average of your Midsemester Reflection grade and this Final Reflection grade.
Remember: Your instructors may adjust your self-assigned grade based on the persuasiveness of your argument and alignment with the evidence you provide.

Guidelines for a Strong Reflection

Reflect on Growth

Compare where you started (Baseline Reflection) to where you are now. What changed? What did you learn about yourself as a learner?

Be Comprehensive

Address the entire semester, not just the second half. Show how your work built on earlier foundations.

Be Specific

Don't just say "I worked hard" — reference specific features of your project, challenges you solved, techniques you learned.

Be Honest

Acknowledge both achievements and limitations. Honest self-assessment demonstrates maturity and metacognition.

Use Evidence

Your Skills Portfolio and Semester Project are your evidence. Reference specific work, decisions, and outcomes.

Make a Case

Your grade argument should be persuasive and supported by the evidence you've provided throughout your reflection.

About Ungrading

This course uses an ungrading model to shift focus from points to learning. Here's how your final grade is determined:

  • Individual assignments receive qualitative feedback (questions, comments, suggestions) rather than scores
  • Your final course grade is the average of two self-assigned grades:
    • Midsemester Reflection grade (Weeks 1-8)
    • Final Reflection grade (Weeks 9-16)
  • You argue for your grade based on work quality, engagement, growth, and timeliness
  • Instructors may adjust your self-assigned grade if the argument doesn't align with the evidence
Example: If you earned a B for Weeks 1-8 (Midsemester) and an A for Weeks 9-16 (Final), your final course grade would be a B+ or A- depending on rounding.
