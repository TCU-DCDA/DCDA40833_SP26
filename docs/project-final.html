Semester Project

Turning In Your Final Project

Note: This page is a draft and its content is subject to revision.

Overview

This page covers everything you need to know about submitting the final version of your Semester Project. Please review the formatting, citation, and general checks below before submitting.

Due: Finals Week

Submit your final project by the end of your section's final exam period:

  • 9:30 section — Tuesday 5/5 by 10:30 AM
  • 11:00 section — Tuesday 5/5 by 1:30 PM
  • 2:00 section — Thursday 5/7 by 4:30 PM

What to Submit

Please submit the following to the D2L dropbox:

1. Project URL

A Word document containing the live URL for your project.

2. Project ZIP File

A ZIP file of your complete project folder.

3. Video Walkthrough

Your 5-minute video walkthrough (or a shareable link). See the Video Walkthrough page for full details.

Formatting

Before you submit, make sure your project's written content and visual presentation meet the following standards:

Scannability

The written content of your site has been formatted effectively to enhance scannability, as described in this short suite of videos: Writing for the Web.

Visual Elements

Each page or section of your site contains at least one visual element (image, graph, illustration, etc.) per page/section to quickly distinguish one page's or section's content from another.

Citation

Proper citation is essential. Make sure your project meets the following requirements:

Works Cited

Your project includes a Works Cited section or page that lists all sources (articles, images, illustrations, video clips). Your Works Cited should link the text of each citation to the original source for all material found online.

Citation Style

Use either APA or MLA as your citation style. Don't worry about the hanging indent — instead, double space between entries.

Internal Citations

Use live links to cite sources internally — identify sources referenced in the body of your content by linking directly to them.

General Checks

Before submitting, take the time to thoroughly review your project:

Cross-Device Testing

View your live site on several machines that are not your own to make sure the site displays properly in a variety of environments.

Navigation

Confirm that navigation is fully functioning on all pages of your site. Test this on several machines.

Embedded Content

Make sure all embedded content (interactive charts, embedded video, etc.) is displaying and functioning properly.

Proofreading

All written content has been thoroughly proofread for any unintentional spelling or grammatical errors.

Showcase Your Work

If you would like to showcase your work on the TCU Center for Digital Expression website (cdex.tcu.edu), please complete the permission form to give us your permission.
