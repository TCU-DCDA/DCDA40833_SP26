Overview

By the deadline, please type up and turn in to this dropbox your answers to the following questions. Please address each point in ample and specific detail.

Due: Sunday, March 15 by 11:30 PM

Submit your Project Proposal and Annotated Bibliography to the D2L dropbox.

Not sure where to start? Try the AI Brainstorming Activity to develop and refine your research question before writing your proposal.

Choose Your Path

To begin, please let us know which option best describes the project you're proposing here. In short, what kind of "deep dive" do you (or your group) plan to take:

Option 1: Data Analysis (DA)

A deep dive into Python-driven data analysis and visualization on a topic of your choosing. You may use WordPress or another web-based template to deliver your results.

Option 2: Digital Culture (DC)

A deep dive into web development (or another multimodal authoring tool) focused on a research topic or creative endeavor that engages some aspect of digital culture. This option does not require strict data analysis.

Research Question (or Research Statement) & Hypothesis

Please state as clearly as you can the most recent version of your Research Question, making reference to the work you discuss in your Annotated Bibliography (see below).

If it helps to more fully articulate your intentions for the project, you are free to write a Research Statement (in paragraph form) that describes your topic in greater detail than a single sentence would allow.

Hypothesis

Your Hypothesis will be "a tentative explanation for an observation, phenomenon, or [cultural or] scientific problem that can be tested by further investigation." In other words, your Hypothesis is your best answer to your Research Question at this early stage of the project. The purpose of the project will be to test the validity of your Hypothesis.

Research Methodology

What specific techniques and methods do you plan to use for this project? Are they mostly quantitative? Mostly qualitative? A combination (hybrid) of both? How will those techniques and methods help you answer your Research Question?

The sources you've gathered during your research will likely be a good resource and reference point here too.

Purpose

In addition to completing the DCDA minor, what do you hope to accomplish with this project? To entertain your audience? To inform/educate them? To persuade them? To call them to action? What are the intended outcomes of your project?

Audience

Who are the various stakeholders (that is, who would have a stake in what you're trying to investigate)? Of the broader set of stakeholders, who is your best audience? In other words, who are you most trying to reach with your findings? Who would most benefit from what you're studying?

What are their stakes in what you have to say? Why should they care? What do they value? What do they want or fear? What kind of information would be most useful for them? What do they already know about the subject?

Exigency

Why is it important to investigate your topic now? Why is the issue a matter of particular, pressing, or even urgent concern?

Annotated Bibliography

With your Project Proposal, please also include an Annotated Bibliography (of a minimum of 10 sources). Aim for 100–150 words per annotation.

Resource: For a helpful discussion of how to compose (and why it's a good thing to write) an annotated bibliography, see the Purdue OWL guide on Annotated Bibliographies.

First, state as a complete sentence the most up-to-date version of your Research Question.

Then, for each article or source, please address the following (whenever appropriate):

  • How does the article or source connect to your possible topic / current Research Question?
  • How does the article clarify or expand on its particular topic? What new insights does it bring to the conversation on the topic?
  • What does the article or source make available in terms of 1) other resources (e.g. other useful works cited by the article or source you're evaluating), 2) its methods, 3) access to source code, and/or 4) access to the original datasets?
  • What questions does the article raise for further investigation? What doors does it open for additional inquiry? What room does it make for your contribution to the discussion?
  • What are the limits of the article (in terms of its findings, its use of its sources, etc.)? What, if any, are the article's shortcomings?
  • How would you plan to use or incorporate this article or source into your Semester Project? What would this source help you accomplish in terms of developing your Semester Project?
