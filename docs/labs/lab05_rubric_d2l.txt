Lab 5 Rubric -- D2L Updates (7 changes)


DIMENSION 1: Experiment Ambition & Effort

Exceeds, bullet 3:
OLD: Shows evidence of iterating with the AI -- refining prompts, trying different approaches, not just accepting the first output
NEW: Shows evidence of trying multiple approaches or pushing past initial results, not settling for the first attempt

Meets, bullet 3:
OLD: Shows evidence of following the prescribed process (plan, prompt, review, test, refine)
NEW: Shows evidence of following the prescribed process (planning, generating, reviewing, testing, and refining)

DNME, bullet 3:
OLD: No evidence of following the process -- jumped straight to accepting AI output
NEW: No evidence of following the process -- no planning, testing, or iteration evident


DIMENSION 3: Critical Reflection

Intro text:
OLD: This evaluates the depth of your thinking about AI as a coding collaborator -- not just what happened, but what it means. The reflection must be your own writing, not AI-generated.
NEW: This evaluates the depth of your thinking about AI as a coding collaborator -- not just what happened, but what it means. The reflection must be your own writing, not AI-generated. (Code comments are evaluated separately in Dimension 2.)

Exceeds, bullet 1:
OLD: Technical Understanding (prompts 1-3): Explains code in their own words with specificity; modifications are described with clear reasoning, not just "I changed a few things"
NEW: Technical Understanding (prompts 1-3): Reflects on the experience of working with AI-generated code with specificity; modifications are described with clear reasoning about why changes were needed, not just what was changed


DIMENSION 4: Process Transparency

DNME, bullet 4:
OLD: Documentation suggests the student accepted AI output without review or iteration
NEW: Submission lacks the artifacts needed to understand the collaboration (prompts, original outputs, revision history)


DIMENSION 5: Writing Quality & Voice

DNME, bullet 2:
OLD: Reads as AI-generated -- polished surface with no personal voice or specific experience
NEW: Lacks personal voice -- reads as generic or formulaic with no connection to specific experience
