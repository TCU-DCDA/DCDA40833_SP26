# Lab 3: Visualization Critique - Evaluation Rubric

## Overview
This rubric uses an ungrading approach to help you understand expectations and assess your own work. Focus on growth, engagement, and critical thinking rather than points.

> **Note for future review:** The Lab 4 rubric introduces a hybrid structure that separates binary completion items (done/not done) from quality dimensions (Exceeds/Meets/Does Not Meet). Consider adopting this approach here — portfolio implementation in particular is largely binary and may not benefit from tiered quality evaluation.
>
> Also: The self-assessment questions in Lab 3's HTML are generic (Depth of Analysis, Evidence & Examples, etc.). Replace with lab-specific questions that are harder to dodge — see Lab 4's rubric and HTML for examples (e.g., "Did I reverse-engineer my expectations?"). Questions should force honest reflection, not just confirm completion.

## 1. Visualization Selection & Source Engagement

### Exceeds Expectations
- Selects a visualization that offers rich opportunities for critical analysis
- Demonstrates thorough engagement with source material, documentation, or artistic statement
- Shows evidence of exploring multiple visualizations before selecting one
- Articulates clear reasons for the selection that connect to course concepts

### Meets Expectations
- Selects an appropriate visualization from one of the provided galleries
- Engages with available source material or documentation
- Selection provides adequate opportunity for meaningful critique
- Basic rationale for selection is evident

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Visualization is not from provided sources or is inappropriate for assignment goals
- Little to no engagement with source material or context
- Selection lacks substance for critical analysis
- No clear rationale for selection provided

---

## 2. Application of Analytical Framework

### Exceeds Expectations
- Demonstrates sophisticated understanding of Tufte's principles (or artistic evaluation criteria)
- Applies concepts precisely and with nuance to specific elements of the visualization
- Makes insightful connections between theory and visual choices
- Goes beyond surface-level observation to uncover deeper implications
- Successfully integrates multiple analytical perspectives

### Meets Expectations
- Demonstrates solid understanding of the chosen analytical framework
- Applies concepts appropriately to the visualization
- Makes clear connections between theory and practice
- Addresses all required components (information resolution + two additional concerns)
- Analysis is clear and grounded in the visualization

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Misunderstands or misapplies analytical concepts
- Analysis is vague or disconnected from the visualization
- Missing required components of the framework
- Relies on generic statements rather than specific observations
- Framework feels tacked on rather than integral to the critique

---

## 3. Critical Thinking & Argumentation

### Exceeds Expectations
- Develops original insights that go beyond obvious observations
- Presents a compelling, well-reasoned argument with strong evidence
- Considers multiple perspectives and potential counterarguments
- Identifies subtle strengths and weaknesses in the visualization
- Makes sophisticated judgments about effectiveness and integrity
- Connects the critique to broader issues in data visualization or communication

### Meets Expectations
- Presents clear, logical arguments supported by evidence
- Identifies both strengths and weaknesses in the visualization
- Makes reasonable judgments about effectiveness
- Analysis demonstrates genuine engagement with the work
- Critique shows independent thinking rather than just summarizing

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Arguments are unclear, unsupported, or contradictory
- Analysis is purely descriptive without critical evaluation
- Fails to identify meaningful strengths or weaknesses
- Critique reads as superficial or rushed
- Shows little evidence of independent thinking

---

## 4. Writing Quality & Depth

### Exceeds Expectations
- Significantly exceeds 500-word minimum with substantive content (not filler)
- Writing is clear, precise, and engaging
- Arguments are well-organized and easy to follow
- Uses specific examples and evidence effectively
- Demonstrates careful revision and attention to craft
- Technical terminology is used accurately and appropriately

### Meets Expectations
- Meets or exceeds 500-word requirement
- Writing is clear and generally well-organized
- Ideas are communicated effectively
- Uses appropriate examples to support points
- Few distracting errors in grammar or mechanics
- Shows evidence of proofreading

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Falls short of 500-word requirement
- Writing is unclear, disorganized, or difficult to follow
- Ideas are underdeveloped or repetitive
- Lacks specific examples or evidence
- Significant errors in grammar, mechanics, or formatting interfere with meaning
- Appears rushed or unrevised

## 5. Portfolio Implementation

### Exceeds Expectations
- Portfolio page shows creativity and polish beyond basic requirements
- HTML structure is clean, semantic, and well-organized
- Styling is thoughtful and enhances readability
- Visualization is properly embedded with appropriate sizing and quality
- Page integrates seamlessly with rest of portfolio
- Navigation is intuitive and links work correctly
- Shows attention to detail and user experience

### Meets Expectations
- Creates functional `lab03.html` page with all required elements
- HTML structure is valid and properly formatted
- Visualization is embedded and clearly visible
- Source link is present and functional
- Page is linked from main portfolio navigation
- Styling is consistent with portfolio aesthetic
- Page displays correctly in browser

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Portfolio page is missing or incomplete
- HTML structure has significant errors
- Visualization is missing, broken, or improperly displayed
- Missing required elements (source link, navigation, etc.)
- Page is not accessible from portfolio navigation
- Significant display or functionality issues
- Work was not pushed to GitHub or published to GitHub Pages

## 6. Engagement with Assignment Options

### Exceeds Expectations (for students using Option A)
- Thoughtfully adapts Tufte's framework to work with chosen visualization type
- Shows flexibility in applying analytical principles
- Makes meaningful distinctions between journalistic and artistic approaches
- Demonstrates understanding of when traditional frameworks need adaptation

### Exceeds Expectations (for students using Option B)
- Shows sophisticated understanding of artistic data visualization
- Effectively balances aesthetic and informational analysis
- Makes insightful observations about data translation and representation
- Demonstrates critical engagement with the relationship between art and data

### Meets Expectations
- Chooses appropriate option for selected visualization
- Follows framework guidelines for chosen option
- Demonstrates adequate understanding of evaluation approach
- Applies criteria consistently throughout critique

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Uses inappropriate framework for selected visualization
- Fails to follow guidelines for chosen option
- Demonstrates confusion about evaluation approach
- Inconsistent or contradictory application of criteria

## Notes on Ungrading Approach

This rubric is designed to help you:
- Understand what high-quality work looks like
- Identify areas of strength and growth in your own work
- Self-assess before submission
- Engage in meaningful reflection about your learning

When you submit your work, you'll have the opportunity to reflect on which categories you feel you've achieved. The goal is developing your critical thinking and technical skills, not just earning a grade.
