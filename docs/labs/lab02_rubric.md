# Lab 2: AI Experimentation & Evaluation - Evaluation Rubric

## Overview
This rubric uses an ungrading approach to help you understand expectations for critical engagement with AI tools. The lab offers two approaches—structured framework (Option A) or open experimentation (Option B)—which are evaluated using adapted criteria.

> **Note for future review:** The Lab 4 rubric introduces a hybrid structure that separates binary completion items (done/not done) from quality dimensions (Exceeds/Meets/Does Not Meet). Consider adopting this approach here — items like portfolio implementation and evidence/documentation may work better as checklist items rather than tiered categories.
>
> Also: Lab 2 has no self-assessment questions. When adding them, develop lab-specific questions that are harder to dodge — see Lab 4's rubric and HTML for examples (e.g., "Did I reverse-engineer my expectations?"). Avoid generic prompts like "Completeness" or "Technical Execution" that students can check off without genuine reflection.

## 1. Tool Selection & Exploration Depth

### Exceeds Expectations
- Explores at least two tools from different categories
- At least one tool is genuinely new to the student
- Pushes tools to their limits; tests edge cases and failure modes
- Documents exploration process thoroughly (screenshots, prompts, outputs)
- Shows curiosity and genuine desire to understand capabilities and limitations
- Experiments are thoughtful and reveal meaningful insights

### Meets Expectations
- Explores at least two tools from different categories
- At least one tool is new or used in a new way
- Tests basic capabilities and some limitations
- Provides evidence of hands-on experimentation
- Documentation shows adequate engagement with tools
- Experiments are relevant to prompt requirements

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Explores fewer than two tools or tools from same category
- Only uses tools already very familiar
- Minimal testing; surface-level engagement
- Little to no documentation of process
- Experiments are cursory or appear rushed
- Does not demonstrate genuine exploration

## 2. Application of Evaluation Framework (Option A) OR Depth of Experimentation (Option B)

### Exceeds Expectations (Option A - Framework)
- Systematically applies all four dimensions (Capabilities, Appropriate Use, Ethics, Transparency) to each tool
- Analysis is specific, nuanced, and supported by concrete examples
- Makes insightful connections between framework dimensions
- Framework application reveals genuine understanding of evaluation criteria
- Identifies subtle distinctions in tool capabilities and appropriate use cases

### Exceeds Expectations (Option B - Experimentation)
- Demonstrates creative, open-ended experimentation
- Probes tools in unexpected or innovative ways
- Documents a clear experimental process with hypothesis-testing approach
- Discoveries are original and go beyond obvious observations
- Shows intellectual curiosity and willingness to take risks

### Meets Expectations (Framework or Experimentation)
- **Framework:** Addresses all four dimensions with adequate specificity and examples
- **Experimentation:** Demonstrates genuine hands-on exploration with documented process
- Analysis is grounded in actual use of the tools
- Makes clear observations about capabilities and limitations
- Provides sufficient evidence to support claims

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- **Framework:** Missing dimensions or analysis is vague and generic
- **Experimentation:** Exploration is superficial or poorly documented
- Claims lack supporting evidence
- Analysis could apply to any tool; not specific to those explored
- Shows minimal engagement with evaluation criteria

## 3. Critical Thinking & Analysis

### Exceeds Expectations
- Develops original insights that go beyond surface observations
- Identifies biases, assumptions, or limitations that aren't immediately obvious
- Makes thoughtful connections between AI capabilities and broader implications
- Considers multiple perspectives (user, developer, society)
- Questions assumptions and interrogates tool claims
- Demonstrates sophisticated understanding of AI's role in DCDA contexts
- Balances skepticism with appreciation for genuine capabilities

### Meets Expectations
- Presents clear analysis supported by evidence from exploration
- Identifies both strengths and limitations of tools
- Shows critical engagement rather than just description
- Considers ethical implications appropriately
- Makes reasonable judgments about appropriate use cases
- Demonstrates independent thinking

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Analysis is purely descriptive; lacks critical evaluation
- Fails to identify meaningful limitations or ethical concerns
- Uncritically accepts tool marketing claims
- Shows no evidence of independent thinking
- Analysis is superficial or rushed
- Makes sweeping claims without supporting evidence

---

## 4. Ethical Awareness & Considerations

### Exceeds Expectations
- Demonstrates sophisticated awareness of ethical issues (bias, transparency, attribution, etc.)
- Goes beyond generic concerns to identify specific issues with explored tools
- Considers impact on multiple stakeholders
- Examines how developers address (or fail to address) ethical concerns
- Thoughtfully discusses responsible use and disclosure practices
- Shows understanding of nuance (AI ethics isn't black and white)

### Meets Expectations
- Addresses ethical considerations appropriately for explored tools
- Identifies relevant concerns about bias, transparency, or appropriate use
- Shows awareness of attribution and citation requirements
- Demonstrates responsible approach to AI use
- Ethical thinking is integrated into analysis (not just tacked on)

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Minimal or no consideration of ethical issues
- Ethical discussion is generic or superficial
- Shows no awareness of bias, attribution, or transparency concerns
- Does not consider responsible use practices
- Ethical section appears as afterthought

## 5. Connection to DCDA Context & Future Application

### Exceeds Expectations
- Makes insightful connections between AI tools and DCDA program skills
- Articulates what remains uniquely human in digital culture/data analytics work
- Develops thoughtful framework for evaluating future AI tools
- Shows understanding of how rapidly evolving tools affect the field
- Considers both opportunities and risks for professional practice
- Demonstrates forward-thinking about AI's role in their career

### Meets Expectations
- Addresses how AI tools relate to DCDA coursework
- Considers appropriate and inappropriate uses in academic/professional context
- Develops basic questions for evaluating new AI tools in future
- Shows reasonable understanding of AI's impact on the field
- Reflection demonstrates engagement with "looking forward" questions

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Minimal or no connection to DCDA context
- Does not address future application or evaluation strategies
- Shows little awareness of AI's relevance to digital culture/data analytics
- Missing "looking forward" reflection
- Analysis remains disconnected from course goals

## 6. Writing Quality & Depth

### Exceeds Expectations
- Significantly exceeds 500-word minimum with substantive content
- Writing is clear, engaging, and well-organized
- Arguments flow logically and build on each other
- Uses specific examples and evidence effectively
- Technical concepts explained accurately
- Shows careful revision and attention to craft
- Voice is professional yet authentic

### Meets Expectations
- Meets or exceeds 500-word minimum (750 words for Option A)
- Writing is clear and well-organized
- Ideas are communicated effectively
- Uses examples to support points
- Few distracting errors in grammar or mechanics
- Shows evidence of revision
- Appropriate tone for academic/professional audience

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Falls significantly short of word count requirement
- Writing is unclear, disorganized, or difficult to follow
- Ideas are underdeveloped or repetitive
- Lacks supporting examples
- Significant errors interfere with communication
- Appears rushed or unrevised
- Inappropriate tone

## 8. Evidence & Documentation

### Exceeds Expectations
- Provides rich documentation of exploration process
- Screenshots are well-chosen, clearly labeled, and integrated meaningfully
- Saves and shares specific prompts and outputs
- Documents both successes and failures
- Evidence directly supports analytical claims
- Creates compelling narrative through documentation

### Meets Expectations
- Provides adequate evidence of hands-on exploration
- Includes screenshots or examples where appropriate
- Documentation supports main points
- Shows genuine engagement with tools
- Evidence is relevant and clearly presented

### Does Not Meet Expectations
- Little to no evidence of hands-on exploration
- Missing screenshots or examples
- Evidence doesn't support claims
- Documentation is unclear or poorly integrated
- Appears to rely on secondary sources rather than direct exploration

## Self-Assessment Questions

As you complete this assignment, consider:

1. **Genuine Exploration**: Did I truly experiment with these tools, or just skim the surface?

2. **Critical Engagement**: Did I move beyond describing what tools do to evaluating how well they do it and considering implications?

3. **Evidence**: Can someone reading my analysis see what I actually tried and discovered?

4. **Ethical Awareness**: Did I thoughtfully consider responsible use, or treat ethics as a checkbox?

5. **Future Application**: Do I have a better sense of how to evaluate AI tools I encounter in the future?

6. **Portfolio Quality**: Would I be proud to show this page to a potential employer?

---

## Notes on Ungrading Approach

This rubric is designed to help you:
- Develop critical evaluation skills that will serve you as AI tools continue evolving
- Move beyond uncritical acceptance or dismissal of AI capabilities
- Practice responsible, thoughtful engagement with emerging technologies
- Build portfolio content that demonstrates analytical thinking

Remember: The goal isn't to become an AI expert, but to develop a framework for critically evaluating new tools as they emerge. Focus on genuine exploration, thoughtful analysis, and honest reflection about both opportunities and limitations.
