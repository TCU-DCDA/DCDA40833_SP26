Data Visualization in Tableau Public

Exploratory Data Analysis

Overview

In this assignment, you will use Tableau Public (a free data visualization platform) to perform exploratory data analysis and visualize a dataset of your choosing.

You can either use the web-based interface or download Tableau Desktop Public Edition.

Getting Started

Tableau has excellent tutorials to help you get familiar with the platform. Work through these to build a foundation:

  • A Beginner's Guide to Tableau Public
  • Getting Started with Tableau Public (Video)
  • How-To Videos
  • Viz Type Tutorials

These will walk you through connecting to a data source, navigating the workspace, creating basic charts, maps, and tables, etc.

Task

Use Tableau Public to identify patterns (and breaks in patterns) in a dataset you choose to visualize.

Dataset Options

  1. A sample dataset provided by Tableau Public (not the one from the tutorial)
  2. A dataset from Kaggle
  3. A dataset you compiled yourself using Instant Data Scraper
  4. Another dataset you have access to
Important: Choose a dataset from a topic you're fairly familiar with.

Reflection & Analysis (500+ words)

Write an analysis addressing the following:

1. Data Preparation

Discuss any errors you received trying to upload your dataset and what you needed to do to prepare it for a successful upload.

2. Design Choices

Describe the choices you made in terms of the visualization's metrics and dimensions.

3. Expected Patterns

Considering your metrics and dimensions, describe the patterns or trends you expected to see based on your understanding of the topic. How much of your understanding is confirmed by the visualization? Focus on a particular section of the graph.

4. Surprises/Outliers

Describe where the visualization deviates from your expectations. Focus on sections with "outliers" or surprises you didn't expect.

5. Questions for Further Investigation

Draft questions that would help explain the outliers/surprises. The goal is not to offer definitive explanations, but to frame questions for deeper investigation.

6. Tool Evaluation

Evaluate Tableau Public as a visualization tool. What can it do that other tools cannot? What can other tools accomplish that Tableau Public cannot (at least as easily)? What limitations did you encounter?

Your reflection should be at least 500 words total.

Create Portfolio Page

Create a new HTML page in your portfolio called tableau-visualization.html that includes:

  • Your published Tableau visualization (embedded using Tableau's embed code)
  • Your reflection and analysis (500+ words addressing all prompts above)
  • Proper HTML structure and styling consistent with your portfolio

Make sure to link this new page from your main portfolio index.html navigation.

Tip: After publishing your viz to Tableau Public, click the "Share" button to get the embed code for your HTML page.
