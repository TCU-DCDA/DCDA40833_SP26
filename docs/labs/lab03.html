Tufte Critique

Evaluating Data Visualization

Overview

Let's apply some of the points from Dr. Crotty's recent lecture (based on Edward Tufte's ideas) to critically evaluate real-world data visualizations. This exercise will sharpen your ability to assess the quality and integrity of visual arguments—a crucial skill for both consuming and creating data-driven content.

Step 1: Select an Infographic

Find one example from the "Beautiful News" section of the Information is Beautiful site:

https://informationisbeautiful.net/beautifulnews/

Read closely one infographic of your choice and its supporting article (provided as a link below the graphic).

Step 2: Write Your Critique (500+ words)

Analyze the visualization using Tufte's framework:

Required Analysis

Critique the visualization in terms of what Tufte describes as information "resolution" (169-176). Put another way, how comprehensive and complete is the graphic regarding the information it provides? Is there important information from the article you feel is missing in the graphic?

Additional Concerns (choose at least two)

  • Effects without causes
  • Cherry-picking
  • Overreaching
  • Rage to Conclude

Your critique should be at least 500 words and demonstrate close reading of both the infographic and its source material.

Step 3: Create Portfolio Page

Create a new HTML page in your portfolio called tufte-critique.html that includes:

  • Your written critique
  • The infographic itself (embedded as an image)
  • A link to the original source
  • Proper HTML structure and styling consistent with your portfolio

Make sure to link this new page from your main portfolio index.html navigation.

Submission

In-Class Discussion

Be prepared to share and discuss your critique with the class. You'll be asked to present your findings and engage in dialogue about how Tufte's principles apply to contemporary data visualization.
