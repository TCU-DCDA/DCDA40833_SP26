Video Walkthrough

Semester Project — Final Presentation

Overview

In lieu of in-class presentations, you will record a 5-minute video walkthrough of your Semester Project. This video is your opportunity to guide your audience through both the content of your research and the deliverable you've built — narrating the choices you made, the story your project tells, and the work that went into it.

Group projects: If you are working in a group, every member must have a speaking role in the video. Divide the walkthrough so that each person presents a portion of the project.

Due: Finals Week

Submit your video by the end of your section's final exam period:

  • 9:30 section — Tuesday 5/5 by 10:30 AM
  • 11:00 section — Tuesday 5/5 by 1:30 PM
  • 2:00 section — Thursday 5/7 by 4:30 PM

What to Cover

Your video should walk your audience through your project by addressing the following. You don't need to treat these as a rigid checklist — think of them as guideposts for a cohesive narrative about your work.

The Content

  • Research Question & Hypothesis — What did you set out to investigate, and what did you expect to find?
  • Methodology — How did you approach the project? What tools, techniques, or methods did you use?
  • Findings & Insights — What did you discover? Did your findings support or challenge your hypothesis?
  • Reflection — What would you do differently? What surprised you? What did you learn?

The Deliverable

  • Walk us through it — Share your screen and navigate through your website, dashboard, visualization, or other artifact as you talk.
  • Design choices — Why did you organize or present your work the way you did? What decisions shaped the final product?
  • Audience & purpose — How does the deliverable serve your intended audience and fulfill the purpose of your project?

Recording Guidelines

Length

Aim for approximately 5 minutes. A minute or two over is fine, but try not to exceed 7 minutes. Practice once or twice to get a feel for pacing — you'll be surprised how quickly 5 minutes goes.

Format

Record a screen share with voiceover narration. You're welcome to include your webcam as well, but it's not required. The key is that we can see your deliverable on screen while you walk us through it.

Recording Tools

Zoom is a straightforward option — start a meeting by yourself, share your screen, hit Record, and present. You're also welcome to use any tool you're comfortable with (e.g., Loom, OBS, QuickTime, or your platform's built-in screen recorder).

Tips

  • Find a quiet space and test your microphone before recording.
  • Have your deliverable open and ready to go before you start.
  • Speak at a natural pace — it's a walkthrough, not a race.
  • It's okay to do multiple takes. Submit the one you're happiest with.

Submission

Upload your video to the D2L dropbox. If the file is too large to upload directly, you may upload it to a cloud service (Google Drive, YouTube as Unlisted, etc.) and submit the link instead. Just make sure the link is accessible — test it in an incognito/private browser window before submitting.
