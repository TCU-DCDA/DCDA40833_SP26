# Writing as Cognition

<div class="mt-8 text-xl leading-relaxed">

Writing is not simply an information delivery system.

</div>

<div class="mt-6 text-2xl font-semibold text-teal-400">
It's a tool of cognition.
</div>

<div class="mt-10 text-base italic opacity-70">
"More than any other single invention, writing has transformed human consciousness."
</div>

# A Test You Can Run

<div class="mt-6 space-y-5 text-base leading-relaxed">

<div class="flex items-start gap-4">
  <span class="bg-teal-500/20 text-teal-400 rounded-full w-8 h-8 flex items-center justify-center font-bold shrink-0">1</span>
  <span>Before you write your next paper, <strong>record yourself</strong> (voice memo, 2 minutes) explaining your main argument</span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-4">
  <span class="bg-teal-500/20 text-teal-400 rounded-full w-8 h-8 flex items-center justify-center font-bold shrink-0">2</span>
  <span>Then <strong>write the paper</strong></span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-4">
  <span class="bg-teal-500/20 text-teal-400 rounded-full w-8 h-8 flex items-center justify-center font-bold shrink-0">3</span>
  <span>Then <strong>compare:</strong> Is the written version the same as what you said? Where did it diverge? Did anything surprise you?</span>
</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-6 bg-teal-500/10 border-l-3 border-teal-400 pl-4 py-3 rounded-r text-sm">
If identical — maybe writing really is transcription for you.<br>
If it diverged, especially in ways you didn't expect — <strong>that's the cognition claim in action.</strong>
</div>

# What Language Actually Does

<div class="mt-6 text-lg leading-relaxed">

Language does more than deliver information. It serves **three functions:**

</div>

<div class="mt-6 grid grid-cols-3 gap-6">
  <div class="bg-teal-500/10 border border-teal-500/20 rounded-lg p-6">
    <div class="text-teal-400 font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Communicate</div>
    <div class="text-sm opacity-80">Deliver information from one person to another</div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-teal-500/10 border border-teal-500/20 rounded-lg p-6">
    <div class="text-teal-400 font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Cognize</div>
    <div class="text-sm opacity-80">You don't fully know your ideas until you've struggled to articulate them</div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-teal-500/10 border border-teal-500/20 rounded-lg p-6">
    <div class="text-teal-400 font-semibold text-lg mb-2">Relate</div>
    <div class="text-sm opacity-80">Negotiate relationships between speaker/writer and hearer/reader</div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="mt-6 text-center text-base opacity-70">
These functions are interconnected — you can't fully separate them.
</div>

<div class="mt-2 text-center font-mono text-xs opacity-40">
Pinker / Nowak
</div>

# The Problem with AI-Generated Prose

<div class="mt-6 space-y-5 text-base leading-relaxed">

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>AI writes via <strong>statistical probability</strong> — what's likely to follow given patterns in training data</span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>This trends toward the <strong>conventional</strong> — the opposite of voice, which is <em>distinctive</em></span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>AI <strong>amplifies</strong> (doesn't create) the existing focus on correctness over engagement</span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>Through AI, you speak in a <strong>collective, standardized voice</strong>, not your own — even if the ideas are yours</span>
</div>

</div>

<div class="bg-teal-500/10 border-l-3 border-teal-400 pl-4 py-3 rounded-r">

# The Relational Dimension

<div class="mt-4 text-xl text-teal-400 italic">
There are times to be efficient, and times where efficiency loses the point.
</div>

<div class="mt-6 space-y-5 text-lg leading-relaxed">

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>Treating writing as information delivery <strong>ignores half its function</strong></span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>"Efficiency" assumes information transfer is primary and relational work is friction — but <strong>the relational work isn't an obstacle to the point; it's part of the point</strong></span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>Academic writing is an occasion to practice <strong>both functions</strong> — working through ideas and positioning yourself in relation to them, to a reader, to a discipline</span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>School is one of the few contexts where someone will actually <strong>read and respond</strong> — a live relational situation</span>
</div>

</div>

---

# The Generalized Performance

<div class="mt-6 space-y-5 text-lg leading-relaxed">

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>Students have been trained to give <strong>"the teacher"</strong> what <strong>"they"</strong> want — a generalized category, not a case-by-case reading</span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>"What the teacher wants" becomes: <em>correct, polished, on time</em></span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>This heuristic has worked — or at least hasn't been penalized</span>
</div>

<div class="flex items-start gap-3">
  <span class="text-teal-400 mt-1">→</span>
  <span>The result: students stop looking for <strong>individual relationships</strong> with individual professors</span>
</div>

</div>

<div class="mt-8 bg-amber-500/10 border-l-3 border-amber-400 pl-4 py-3 rounded-r text-base">
AI-generated prose fits this logic perfectly — it <strong>performs compliance</strong> efficiently.<br>
What's lost: the chance to be <strong>encountered</strong>, to show up, to discover that some professors are reading <em>for them</em>.
</div>

# What We're Actually Asking

<div class="mt-4 text-base opacity-80">

We recognize that writing is harder for some students than others — language barriers, learning differences, five courses and a job. The friction isn't equally distributed, and we don't have a clean answer for where the line is.

</div>

<div class="mt-6 text-lg leading-relaxed">

But we want to distinguish between:

</div>

<div class="mt-4 grid grid-cols-2 gap-6">
  <div class="bg-teal-500/10 border border-teal-500/20 rounded-lg p-6">
    <div class="text-teal-400 font-semibold mb-2">Using tools to overcome barriers</div>
    <div class="text-sm opacity-80">Tools that help you express what you're thinking</div>
  </div>
  <div class="bg-amber-500/10 border border-amber-500/20 rounded-lg p-6">
    <div class="text-amber-400 font-semibold mb-2">Using tools to skip the thinking</div>
    <div class="text-sm opacity-80">Tools that replace the cognitive work altogether</div>
  </div>
</div>

<div class="mt-6 space-y-3 text-lg">
  <div>The point isn't <strong>"never use AI."</strong></div>
  <div>The point is: <strong>know what you're trading when you do.</strong></div>
</div>
