# DCDA 40833: Senior Capstone (Spring 2026)

Welcome to the course repository for the DCDA Senior Capstone. This repository contains all necessary materials, lab instructions, and project guidelines for the semester.

## Course Overview

This capstone course is the culminating experience for the Digital Culture and Data Analytics (DCDA) major and minor. Students will undertake a significant "deep dive" project that synthesizes the skills and knowledge acquired throughout the program.

**Instructors:** Dr. Curt Rode & Dr. Sean Crotty

### Learning Outcomes

1. Apply proper statistical methods for analysis and interpret results
2. Demonstrate ability to compose digital arguments
3. Engage with significant works of scholarship and research methods in digital studies

## Assignments

- **Labs 1-6** — Skill-building exercises (AI tools, HTML/CSS, data visualization, Python mapping)
- **Skills Portfolio** — Web-based showcase of lab work and skills
- **Self-Reflections** — Midsemester & Final reflections with grade justification
- **Project Proposal** — Research question, methodology, and annotated bibliography
- **Semester Project** — Deep dive into a topic (solo or group)

## Semester Project

Students choose between two tracks:

1. **Option 1 (Data Analysis):** A deep dive into Python-driven data analysis and visualization on a topic of your choosing.
2. **Option 2 (Digital Culture):** A deep dive into web development or multimodal authoring focused on a research topic or creative endeavor.

Please refer to the `project/` folder for full details on the proposal, research methodology, and deliverables.

## Grading Philosophy

This course uses an **"ungrading" model** focused on qualitative assessment:

- No numerical grades on individual assignments
- Final grade = average of 2 self-assigned grades (Midsemester + Final Reflection)
- Students argue for their grade based on work quality and engagement
- Instructors may adjust based on persuasiveness of argument

> *"If class time is spent with only the instructors talking, this course is going to suck for all of us."*

## AI Policy

AI tools are **permitted with proper citation**. Students must fact-check AI output and cannot use AI to replace critical thinking.
