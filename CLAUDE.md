## Core Principles

### 1. No Assumptions

- **Do NOT assume** user intent, file changes, or scope of work
- **ASK clarifying questions** before proceeding with ambiguous requests
- When in doubt, ask — don't guess

### 2. Approval Required

- **Make NO changes** beyond what has been explicitly approved
- Propose changes first, implement only after confirmation
- If a request seems to expand scope, pause and verify

### 3. Minimal Intervention

- Change only what is necessary to fulfill the approved request
- Preserve existing content, formatting, and structure unless instructed otherwise
- Document any suggested improvements separately — do not implement without approval

## Course Context: Grading Policy

This repository supports a course using an **"ungrading" model**:

### How Grading Works

- **No numerical grades** on individual assignments
- Students receive qualitative feedback (questions, comments) rather than scores
- **Final grade = average of 2 self-assigned grades:**
  1. **Midsemester Reflection** (covers weeks 1-8)
  2. **Final Reflection** (covers weeks 9-16)

### Student Responsibility

- Students **argue for their grade** based on:
  - Quality of work submitted
  - Engagement and participation
  - Response to feedback
  - Timeliness of submissions
- Instructors may adjust grades based on persuasiveness of argument

### Key Policies

- Late work affects ability to receive timely feedback
- **Late Semester Projects and Final Reflections are NOT accepted** (graduating seniors)
- Attendance is tracked but not graded — affects grade argument persuasiveness

### AI Usage

- AI tools are **permitted with proper citation**
- Students must fact-check AI-generated content
- AI cannot replace critical thinking, judgment, or topic expertise
