## Overview

AI tools are rapidly reshaping how we create, analyze, and communicate. In this lab, you will explore and critically evaluate AI tools relevant to your work as a DCDA student — including text generation, image creation, and code assistance. The goal is not to master any single tool, but to develop a **framework for evaluation** that you can apply as tools evolve.

---

## Part 1: The Evaluation Framework

When assessing any AI tool, consider these dimensions:

### Capabilities
- What can this tool do well?
- What are its limitations (what it can't do, or does poorly)?
- How does output quality vary based on input/prompting?

### Appropriate Use
- What tasks is this tool well-suited for?
- When might using this tool be inappropriate or counterproductive?
- How does it fit into a larger workflow (not replacing human judgment)?

### Ethical Considerations
- How was the tool trained? On what data?
- What biases might be present in its outputs?
- What are the implications for intellectual property, attribution, and authenticity?
- What safeguards exist, and what gaps remain?

### Transparency & Citation
- How do you disclose AI use in your work?
- What does proper attribution look like?
- How do you verify/fact-check AI-generated content?

## Part 2: Hands-On Exploration

Choose **at least two AI tools** from different categories to explore:

### Categories

**Text/Chat (choose one or more):**
- ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot, or similar

**Image Generation (choose one or more):**
- Adobe Firefly, DALL-E, Midjourney, Stable Diffusion, or similar

**Code Assistance (choose one or more):**
- GitHub Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT, Cursor, or similar

### Exploration Tasks

For each tool you explore:

1. **Test its capabilities** — Give it tasks relevant to your interests or coursework
2. **Push its limits** — Find where it struggles or fails
3. **Document your process** — Save prompts, outputs, and observations

---

## Part 3: Reflection & Analysis

Write a **500-750 word analysis** addressing:

### For Each Tool You Explored:

1. **Capabilities:** What did the tool do well? Where did it fall short? Include specific examples (screenshots, copied outputs, etc.)

2. **Appropriate Use:** Based on your exploration, what would be a good use case for this tool? What would be a poor use case?

3. **Ethical Considerations:** What concerns arise from using this tool? How is the developer addressing (or not addressing) these concerns?

### Broader Questions:

4. **Personal Use:** How do you imagine using AI tools appropriately in your own academic and professional work?

5. **DCDA Context:** How might AI tools affect the skills taught in the DCDA program — both positively and negatively? What remains uniquely human in digital culture and data analytics work?

6. **Evolving Landscape:** These tools change rapidly. What questions should you ask when evaluating a *new* AI tool in the future?
