## Overview

Apply principles from Edward Tufte's work to critically evaluate real-world data visualizations. This exercise will sharpen your ability to assess the quality and integrity of visual arguments—a crucial skill for both consuming and creating data-driven content.

## Step 2: Write Your Critique (500+ words)

Choose your analytical approach:

### Option A: Unified Approach
Analyze the visualization using Tufte's framework, adapted for all visualization types.

**Required Analysis:**
- Critique in terms of information **"resolution"** (169-176) — how comprehensive and complete is the graphic?
- If there's accompanying documentation: Is important information missing?
- If it's an artistic interpretation: What additional context would enhance understanding?

**Additional Concerns (choose at least two):**
- **Effects without causes** - Does it show outcomes without explaining what produced them?
- **Cherry-picking** - Has data been selectively chosen?
- **Overreaching** - Do conclusions extend beyond what data supports?
- **Rage to Conclude** - Does it rush to conclusions without sufficient evidence?

### Option B: Artistic Focus
Use this if you selected from Feeling Data Studio or Data to Art.

**Required Analysis: Data Translation & Representation**
- What data is being represented, and how clear is this translation?
- What is gained through artistic interpretation? What might be lost?
- Does the aesthetic approach enhance or obscure meaning?
- How does the work balance artistic expression with informational integrity?

**Additional Critical Perspectives (choose at least two):**
- **Metaphor & Meaning** - Are visual metaphors appropriate?
- **Emotional Manipulation vs. Engagement** - Does emotion deepen understanding or override critical thinking?
- **Accessibility** - Can diverse audiences interpret the visualization?
- **Context & Completeness** - Is sufficient context provided?
